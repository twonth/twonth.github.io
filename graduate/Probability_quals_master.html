Problems

  1. For any random variable \(X\) with finite mean \(\mu\), prove that:
    \[ \min_{c} E\left[(X-c)^2\right] = E\left[(X-\mu)^2\right] = \operatorname{Var}(X). \]
  2. Let \(X\) and \(Y\) be independent random variables with the same exponential density \(f(x)=e^{-x}\) for \(x>0\). Show that the sum \(X+Y\) and the ratio \(X/Y\) are independent.
  3. Let \(\{X_n\}\) be a sequence of i.i.d. random variables such that \(P(|X_1|>c)>0\) for all \(c>0\). Show that:
    \[ P\!\left(\limsup_{n\rightarrow\infty} |X_n|=\infty\right)=1. \]
  4. Let \(\{X_n\}\) be i.i.d. random variables with mean \(\mu\) and finite variance \(\sigma^2\). Let \(S_n=X_1+\cdots+X_n\). Show that:
    \[ \frac{S_n-n\mu}{\sigma\sqrt{n}} \longrightarrow N(0,1) \quad \text{in distribution as } n\rightarrow\infty \]
  5. Let \(\{X_n\}\) be a sequence of i.i.d. random variables with \(E|X_1|<\infty\). Prove that:
    \[ \sum_{n=1}^{\infty} (-1)^n \frac{X_n}{n} \quad \text{converges almost surely.} \]
  6. Let \(\{X_n\}\) be a sequence of i.i.d. random variables with \(E[X_1]=0\) and \(E|X_1|^{\alpha}<\infty\) for some \(\alpha>0\). Prove that for any \(\beta>\alpha\):
    \[ \frac{1}{n^{1/\beta}} \max_{1\leq k\leq n} |X_k| \longrightarrow 0 \quad \text{in probability as } n\rightarrow\infty \]

      \[ E\bigl(X - E[X \mid \mathcal{D}]\bigr)^2 \leq E(X - \xi)^2 \]
      for all \(\mathcal{D}\)-measurable r.v.s \(\xi\).
    2. Let \((\Omega,\mathcal{F},P)\) denote a probability space. Suppose \(f : \mathbb{R}^n \times \Omega \to \mathbb{R}\) is a bounded \(\mathcal{B}(\mathbb{R}^n)\times\mathcal{C}\)-measurable function and \(X\) be a \(n\)-dimensional \(\mathcal{D}\)-measurable random variable. Assume \(\mathcal{C}\) and \(\mathcal{D}\) are independent. If \(g(x) := E f(x,\omega)\), then
      \[ g(X) = E[f(X,\omega)\mid \mathcal{D}], \text{ a.s.} \]
  3. Show that random variables \(X_n, n \geq 1\), and \(X\) satisfy \(X_n \to X\) in distribution iff
    \[ E[F(X_n)] \to E[F(X)] \]
    for every continuous distribution function \(F\).
  4. Let \(\{X_n\}\) be a sequence of iid random variables with \(E|X_1| = \infty\). Let \(S_n = X_1 + X_2 + \cdots + X_n\). Show that

Problems

  1. Cauchyâ€“Schwarz inequality \(\Rightarrow\) for \(0 \le h \le t\)

    \[ \big(E|X|^t\big)^2 \le E\big(|X|^{t+h}\big)\,E\big(|X|^{t-h}\big) \]

    Setting \(t_1=t+h,\ t_2=t-h\)

    \[ \Rightarrow \left(E|X|^{\frac{t_1+t_2}{2}}\right)^2 \le E\big(|X|^{t_1}\big)\,E\big(|X|^{t_2}\big) \]
    \[ \Rightarrow 2\log E|X|^{\frac{t_1+t_2}{2}} \le \log E|X|^{t_1} + \log E|X|^{t_2} \]
    \[ \Rightarrow g\!\left(\frac{t_1+t_2}{2}\right)\le \frac{g(t_1)+g(t_2)}{2} \Rightarrow g(t)\ \text{convex}\ (g(t)\ \text{continuous}) \]

    Note that \(g(0)=0\).

    \[ \frac{g(t)}{t}=\frac{g(t)-g(0)}{t-0}\ \uparrow\ \text{in }t \Rightarrow \big(E|X|^t\big)^{1/t}\uparrow\ \text{in }t \]

  2. If \(X,Y\) independent, then

    \[ E f(X) g(Y)=E f(X)\,E g(Y) \]

    for simple functions \(f,g\)

    \[ \left(f=\sum a_i I_{A_i},\quad g=\sum b_j I_{B_j}\right) \]

    Approximate continuous \(f,g\) by simple functions.

    If \(E f(X)g(Y)=E f(X)E g(Y)\), then choose continuous \(f,g\) to approximate \(I_A\) and \(I_B\).

  3. It suffices to show \(P\!\left(\left|\frac{S_n-np}{n^\beta}\right|>\frac1k\ \text{i.o.}\right)=0,\ \forall k=1,2,\ldots\)

    Let \(S_n^\ast=\dfrac{S_n-np}{\sqrt{npb}}\), then

    \[ P\!\left(\left|\frac{S_n-np}{n^\beta}\right|>\frac1k\ \text{i.o.}\right) =P\!\left(\left|S_n^\ast\right|>\frac{n^{\beta-\frac12}}{k\sqrt{pb}}\ \text{i.o.}\right) \le P\!\left(|S_n^\ast|>2\sqrt{\log n}\ \text{i.o.}\right) \]

    Use normal approximation to obtain

    \[ \begin{aligned} P\!\left(|S_n^\ast|>2\sqrt{\log n}\right) &=P\!\left(S_n^\ast>2\sqrt{\log n}\right)+P\!\left(S_n^\ast<-2\sqrt{\log n}\right) \\ &\sim \frac{1}{2\sqrt{\log n}}\,\varphi\!\left(2\sqrt{\log n}\right) +\frac{1}{2\sqrt{\log n}}\,\varphi\!\left(2\sqrt{\log n}\right) \end{aligned} \]

    where \(\varphi(x)=\dfrac{1}{\sqrt{2\pi}}e^{-x^2/2}\)

    \(\Rightarrow \sum P\!\left(|S_n^\ast|>2\sqrt{\log n}\right)<\infty\)

  4. Take \(a_n=\dfrac{(-1)^n}{n}\). Then \(a_n=O\!\left(\dfrac1n\right)\), \(\sum a_n\) converges.

    Assume \(X_1\ge 0\) (consider \(X_1^+\), \(X_1^-\) separately).

    Set \(X_n' = X_n I_{\{X_n\le n\}}\),

    \[ Y_n=X_n'-E X_n' \]

    Borelâ€“Cantelli theorem \(\Rightarrow X_n',X_n\) equivalent. \(E X_n' \to E X_n \Rightarrow \sum a_n E X_n'\) converges.

    Suffices to show \(\sum a_n Y_n\) converges.

    Set \(A_j=\{j-1

    \[ \begin{aligned} \Rightarrow \sum_n a_n^2 E Y_n^2 &\le \sum_n a_n^2 E(X_n')^2 \le \sum \frac1{n^2}\int_{X_1\le n} X_1^2 \\ &= \sum_n \sum_{j=1}^n \frac1{n^2}\int_{A_j} X_1^2 \le 2 E X_1 \end{aligned} \]

    Khintchineâ€“Kolmogorov convergence theorem \(\Rightarrow \sum a_n Y_n\) converges.

  5. In view of the mean convergence criterion, it suffices to show

    \[ \sup_n E|x_n|^p<\infty \qquad \text{provided } x_n\xrightarrow{P}x. \]

    Pf

    (1) \(\exists \delta_0>0\) s.t. \(P(A)<\delta_0 \Rightarrow \sup_n \int_A |x_n|^p\,dP<1\)

    (2) \(\exists a>0,\exists N_0\) s.t. \(P(|x_n|>a)<\delta_0,\ \forall n>N_0\)

    To see (2), note that \(\exists a_1\), \(P(|x|>a_1)<\frac{\delta_0}{2}\) \([\,\text{b/c }P(|x|<\infty)=1\,]\)

    \(\exists N_0\) s.t. \(P(|x_n|>2a_1)\le P(|x_n-x|\ge a_1)+P(|x|>a_1)<\frac{\delta_0}{2}+\frac{\delta_0}{2}=\delta_0\).

    For \(n\ge N_0\),

    \[ \begin{aligned} E|x_n|^p &=E|x_n|^p I_{\{|x_n|\ge a\}}+E\!\left(|x_n|^p I_{\{|x_n|\le a\}}\right) \\ &\le 1+a^p \\ \Rightarrow\ &\sup_n E|x_n|^p<\infty \end{aligned} \]

  6. Choose \(n_m=\) smaller integer \(\ge m^{2/\alpha}\) \((m=1,2,\ldots)\)

    Then \(Y_m:=\dfrac{1}{n_m}(X_1+\cdots+X_{n_m})\xrightarrow{\text{a.s.}}0\) as \(m\to\infty\)

    In fact \(E(Y_m)^2\le \dfrac{c}{n_m^\alpha}\le \dfrac{c}{m^2}\) (for some \(c\))

    \[ \begin{aligned} P\!\left(\max_{m\ge N}|Y_m|>\varepsilon\right) &\le P\!\left(\bigcup_{m\ge N}|Y_m|>\varepsilon\right) \\ &\le P(|Y_N|>\varepsilon)+\cdots \\ &\le \frac1{\varepsilon^2}\left(E(Y_N)^2+\cdots\right)\to 0 \quad \text{as }N\to\infty \end{aligned} \]

    Set

    \[ Z_m=\max_{1\le k\le n_{m+1}-n_m}\left|\frac{X_{n_m+1}+\cdots+X_{n_m+k}}{n_m+k}\right| \]

    Then \(\left|\dfrac{X_1+\cdots+X_n}{n}\right|\le Y_m+Z_m\) provided \(n_m\le n\le n_{m+1}\).

    Now

    \[ \begin{aligned} E Z_m^2 &\le E\!\left(\frac{n_{m+1}-n_m}{n_m^2}\sum_{j=1}^{n_{m+1}-n_m} X_{n_m+j}^2\right) \\ &\le \left(\frac{n_{m+1}-n_m}{n_m}\right)^2 \le \left(\frac{(m+1)^{2/\alpha}-m^{2/\alpha}}{m^{2/\alpha}}\right)^2 \sim \frac{c}{m^2}\quad (\text{if }1<\alpha<2) \end{aligned} \]

    If \(\alpha>2\), take \(\alpha<\beta<2\), \(\alpha=\sigma+\beta\) \((\sigma>0)\), then \(E\!\left(\frac1n(X_1+\cdots+X_n)\right)^2=O\!\left(\frac1{n^\beta}\right)\).

    \(\Rightarrow |Z_m|^2\le \dfrac{c}{m^2}\Rightarrow Z_m\xrightarrow{\text{a.s.}}0\)

    \(\Rightarrow \dfrac{X_1+\cdots+X_n}{n}\xrightarrow{\text{a.s.}}0\).

    1. \(E[M_t-M_s\mid \mathcal F_s]\) for \(t\ge s\), \((\mathcal F_t=\mathcal F_t^B)\)

      \[ \begin{aligned} &=E[B_t^2-B_s^2-t+s\mid \mathcal F_s] \\ &=E\!\left[(B_t-B_s)^2+2B_s(B_t-B_s)-t+s\mid \mathcal F_s\right] \\ &=E[(B_t-B_s)^2\mid \mathcal F_s]+2B_sE[B_t-B_s\mid \mathcal F_s]-t+s \\ &=E(B_t-B_s)^2+2B_sE(B_t-B_s)-t+s \\ &=t-s+0-t+s=0 \quad \Rightarrow\ M_t\ \text{is a martingale.} \end{aligned} \]

    2. \(E[N_t-N_s\mid \mathcal F_s]\)

      \[ \begin{aligned} &=E\!\left[(B_t-B_s)^3 - 3(B_t-B_s)^2B_s + 3(B_t-B_s)B_s^2 - 3(t-s)B_s \mid \mathcal F_s\right] \\ &=E(B_t-B_s)^3 - 3B_sE(B_t-B_s)^2 + 3B_s^2E(B_t-B_s) + 3(t-s)B_s + 0 \\ &= -3B_s(t-s)+3(t-s)B_s=0 \Rightarrow N_t\ \text{is a martingale.} \end{aligned} \]
