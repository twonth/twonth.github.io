Analysis Qualifying Exam: real analysis part

January, 2005

Instructions

Do all 5 problems.

Problems

  1. Suppose \(A\) is an uncountable subset of \(\mathbb{R}\) and \(A'\) is the set of limit points of \(A\). Prove \(A \cap A'\) is uncountable.
  2. If \(0 < p \leq 1\), prove that \(\frac{\sin x}{x^p}\) is not integrable on \((0,\infty)\), but \[ \int_0^\infty \frac{\sin x}{x^p}\,dx = \lim_{r\rightarrow\infty} \int_0^r \frac{\sin x}{x^p}\,dx \] exists.
  3. Suppose \(\left\{f_n : n \geq 1\right\}\) is a sequence of continuous functions on \(\mathbb{R}^m\) with the property that for every \(x \in \mathbb{R}^m\), \[ \sum_{n=1}^{\infty} \left|f_n(x) - f_{n-1}(x)\right| \leq \frac{1}{1 + |x|^2}. \] Prove the sequence \(\left\{f_n : n \geq 1\right\}\) converges to a continuous function.
  4. If \(\mu\) is Lebesgue measure on \(\mathbb{R}\) and \(f \in L(\mathbb{R}, d\mu)\), prove that \(F\) is absolutely continuous where \[ F(x) = \int_{(-\infty, x]} f\,d\mu. \]
  5. Let \(f\) be continuously differentiable on \(\mathbb{R}\), and suppose that \(f\) and \(f'\) are both integrable. Prove \[ \lim_{k\rightarrow\infty} \int_{-\infty}^{\infty} \sin(kx)f(x)\,dx = 0. \]

Analysis Qualifying Exam: complex analysis part

January, 2005

Instructions

5 problems, counted 10 points apiece.

Problems

  1. State the most general version that you know of Cauchy's Integral Formula and then sketch a proof of the simplest version of Cauchy's Integral Formula.
  2. Evaluate the following integral by the method of residues (with justification). \[ \int_{-\infty}^{\infty} \frac{1 + x^2}{1 + x^4}\,dx \]
  3. Prove that if a complex power series \(\sum_{n=0}^{\infty} a_n z^n\) converges for some \(z_0 \neq 0\), then it converges absolutely in the open disk \(\left\{z \in \mathbb{C} : |z| < |z_0|\right\}\) and uniformly on any closed subdisk.
  4. State the general theorem on existence of a Laurent series and determine the Laurent series for \[ \frac{z}{1 - z^2} \] on the annulus \(0 < |z - 1| < 1\) and on the annulus \(1 < |z| < \infty\).
  5. Construct a conformal mapping from \(H = \{z \in \mathbb{C} : \operatorname{Im}(z) > 0\}\) in \(\mathbb{C}\) onto \(G = \{w \in \mathbb{C} : \operatorname{Re}(w) > 0\text{ and }|w| < 1\}\). Include a definition of conformal mapping, and indicate why your mapping is conformal.

Problems

  1. Give clearly reasoning and state clearly which theorems you are using.
    1. Prove that \(f(x) = \sum_{n = 1}^{\infty} \frac{x^{n-1}}{n^{2}(1 + x^{n})}\) is continuous for \(x \geq 0\).
    2. Evaluate \(\int_{0}^{1} f(x)\,dx\), justifying all steps of your work, and express your answer in terms of values of the functions \(\zeta(s) = \sum_{n=1}^{\infty} \frac{1}{n^{s}}\).
  2. Let \(f(x) = \frac{\sin x}{x}\). Prove the following statements:
    1. \(\int_{0}^{\infty} |f(x)|\,dx = \infty\).
    2. \(\lim_{b \to \infty} \int_{0}^{b} f(x)\,dx = \frac{\pi}{2}\) by repeated integrating \(e^{-xy}\sin x\) with respect to \(x\) and \(y\).

      Hint: you may need the formula \(\int e^{au}\sin bu\,du = \frac{e^{au}}{a^{2} + b^{2}}(a\sin bu - b\cos bu) + C\).

  3. Let \(E \subset \mathbb{R}\) be a Lebesgue measurable set and \(0 < m(E) < \infty\). Prove that for every \(\epsilon > 0\), there exist a set \(A\) that is a finite union of open intervals such that
    \[ m(E \backslash A) + m(A \backslash E) < \epsilon \]
  4. Suppose \(\mu(X) < \infty\). If \(f\) and \(g\) are complex-valued measurable function on \(X\), define
    \[ \rho(f,g) = \int_{X} \frac{|f-g|}{1 + |f-g|}\,d\mu \]
    Prove that \(\lim_{n \to \infty} \rho(f_{n},f) = 0\) if and only if \(\lim_{n \to \infty} f_{n} = f\) in measure.
  5. Let \((X,\mathcal{M},\mu)\) be a positive measure space with \(\mu(X) < \infty\).
    1. Show that a measurable function \(f : X \to [0,\infty)\) is integrable (i.e., one has \(\int_{X} f(x)\,d\mu < \infty\)) if and only if the series \(\sum_{n=0}^{\infty} \mu(\{x : f(x) \geq n\})\) converges.
    2. Let \(f\) be a nonnegative measurable function on \([0,1]\) satisfying
      \[ m(\{x : f(x) \geq t\}) < \frac{1}{1 + t^{2}}, \quad t > 0 \]
      Using the result in (a) to determine those values of \(p\), \(1 \leq p < \infty\) for which \(f \in L^{p}([0,1])\) and find the minimum value of \(p\) for which \(f\) may fail to be in \(L^{p}\). Give an example.

Instructions

Show all your work and prove all your assertions. To pass, you must demonstrate satisfactory knowledge of both Real Analysis and Complex Analysis.

Real Analysis Questions

  1. Show that if \(E \subset \mathbb{R}\) is uncountable, then there is some \(t \in \mathbb{R}\) such that both \(E \cap (-\infty,t)\) and \(E \cap (t,\infty)\) are uncountable.
  2. Let \(F=(F_1,\ldots,F_n):\mathbb{R}^n \rightarrow \mathbb{R}^n\) be a continuously differentiable map. Assume that \(F(\overrightarrow{0})=\overrightarrow{0}\) and that \[ \sum_{i,j=1}^n \left|\frac{\partial F_i}{\partial x_j}(\overrightarrow{0})\right|^2 < 1. \] Show there is a closed ball \(B\) centered at \(\overrightarrow{0}\) such that \(F(B)\subset B\).
  3. Let \(E \subset [0,1]\times[0,1]\) be a Lebesgue measurable set. Denote the horizontal sections of \(E\) by \(E_y=\{x\in[0,1]:(x,y)\in E\}\) and the vertical sections by \(E^x=\{y\in[0,1]:(x,y)\in E\}\).

    Assume that \(\lvert E_y\rvert \ge 1/2\) for every \(0 \le y \le 1\), where \(\lvert A\rvert\) denotes the Lebesgue measure of a measurable set \(A\).

    Show that the set \(H=\{x\in[0,1]: \lvert E^x\rvert \ge 1/4\}\) is Lebesgue measurable, and that \(\lvert H\rvert \ge 1/3\).

  4. Let \(E \subset \mathbb{R}\) be an open set of finite measure. Define \(f(x)=\lvert E\cap(E+x)\rvert\) for \(x\in\mathbb{R}\). Here \(E+x=\{z+x; z\in E\}\) and \(\lvert A\rvert\) denotes the Lebesgue measure of a set \(A\).

    Show that \(\lim_{x\rightarrow\infty} f(x)=0\) and that \(f\) is uniformly continuous on \(\mathbb{R}\).

  5. Let \((H,\langle\, , \,\rangle)\) be a real Hilbert space; write \(\lVert x\rVert^2=\langle x,x\rangle\). Suppose \(K\subset H\) is a closed convex subset, and that \(x\in H\setminus K\).
    1. Show there is a vector \(z\in K\) such that \(\lVert x-z\rVert \le \lVert x-y\rVert\) for all \(y\in K\).
    2. Show there are a unit vector \(u\in H\) and a constant \(C\) such that \(\langle u,x\rangle < C \le \langle u,y\rangle\) for all \(y\in K\).

Complex Analysis Questions

  1. Exhibit a conformal map from the strip \(\{z\in\mathbb{C}:0<\operatorname{Im}(z)<1\}\) onto the unit disc \(D(0,1)=\{z\in\mathbb{C}: \lvert z\rvert<1\}\).
  2. Compute \[ \int_0^\infty \frac{dx}{(x^2+a^2)^2}, \] where \(a>0\).
  3. Show there is a \(T>0\) such that \(\sum_{n=1}^\infty \frac{1}{2^n}\sin(nz)\) converges uniformly to a function analytic on \(\{z\in\mathbb{C}:-T<\operatorname{Im}(z)<T\}\), and find the largest such \(T\).
  4. For each integer \(n\ge 1\), let \[ P_n(z)=1+z+\frac{1}{2!}z^2+\frac{1}{3!}z^3+\cdots+\frac{1}{n!}z^n. \] Show that for all sufficiently large \(n\), \(P_n(z)\) has no zeros in \(D(0,10)=\{z\in\mathbb{C}:\lvert z\rvert<10\}\), and \(P_n(z)-1\) has exactly 3 zeros there.
  5. Suppose \(f(z)\) is an entire function, and that for each \(a\in\mathbb{C}\) at least one coefficient \(c_n=c_n(a)\) in the Taylor expansion \[ f(z)=\sum_{n=0}^\infty c_n(a)(z-a)^n \] is 0. Show that \(f(z)\) must be a polynomial.

Spring, 2002

Name   Student Id. No.  

Instruction

There are ten problems in total. Please work as many problems as possible. Use a separate sheet of paper to do each problem and show all your work.

Problems

  1. Suppose that \(f\) is \(n\) times continuously differentiable. Show that \[ \begin{aligned} f(x) ={}& f(a) + f'(a)(x-a) + \cdots + \frac{1}{(n-1)!}f^{(n-1)}(a)(x-a)^{n-1} \\ &+ \frac{1}{(n-1)!}\int_a^b f^{(n)}(t)(x-t)_+^n\,dt \end{aligned} \] where \[ (x-t)_+^n = \begin{cases} (x-t)^n, & \text{if } x \ge t,\\ 0, & \text{if } x < t. \end{cases} \]
  2. Suppose that a bounded function \(H(x)\) defined on the real line satisfies that \(H(0)=1\) and \(H(x)\) is continuously differentiable in a neighborhood of \(x=0\). Let \[ \phi(x)=\prod_{j=1}^{\infty} H\!\left(x/2^j\right). \] Show that \(\phi\) is well-defined for any real number \(x\). Furthermore, show that \(\phi\) is a continuous function if \(H(x)\) is a continuous function.
  3. Suppose that \(f,g\in L_2(-\infty,\infty)\) and \(f\) vanishes outside of a bounded closed set. Show that \[ \sum_{k=-\infty}^{\infty} 2^{-n}\left(\int_{-\infty}^{\infty} f(x)g\!\left(2^{-n}x-k\right)\,dx\right)^2 \longrightarrow 0, \] as \(n\to\infty\).
  4. Suppose that \(\left\{f_n\in L_2(0,2\pi),\, n=1,2,\cdots\right\}\) is a sequence which is weakly convergent to \(F\in L_2(0,2\pi)\), i.e., \[ \int_0^{2\pi} \bigl(f_n(x)-F(x)\bigr)h(x)\,dx \longrightarrow 0,\qquad n\to+\infty \] for any \(h\in L_2(0,2\pi)\). Suppose also that \(\|f_n\|_2\to \|F\|_2\) as \(n\to\infty\), where \[ \|f_n\|_2=\sqrt{\int_0^{2\pi} |f_n(x)|^2\,dx} \] and similar for \(\|F\|_2\). Show that \(f_n\) converges to \(F\) in the \(L_2\) norm.
  5. Suppose that \(\phi\) is a continuous function and \[ \sum_{k=-\infty}^{\infty} |\phi(x+k)|^2 = 1,\qquad \forall x\in[0,1]. \] Show that for any \(\epsilon>0\), there exists an integer \(K\) such that \[ \sum_{|k|\le K} |\phi(x+k)|^2 \ge 1-\epsilon,\qquad \forall x\in[0,1]. \]
  6. Use the Rouche theorem to prove the fundamental theorem of algebra.
  7. Compute the Laurent series of function \[ f(z)=\frac{1}{(z^3-1)(z-3)} \] in annulus \(\{z:1<|z|<3\}\).
  8. Use the residue theorem to compute the following integral: \[ I=\int_0^{\infty}\frac{1}{(1+x^2)^2}\,dx. \]
  9. Find explicitly a conformal mapping of \(\{z:0<\operatorname{Im}(z)<\pi\}\) onto the unit disk.
  10. Suppose that \(f\) is a continuous function on \((-\infty,\infty)\) which vanishes outside of a bounded closed set. Define \[ \hat f(z)=\int_{-\infty}^{\infty} f(t)\exp(-izt)\,dt,\qquad \forall z\in \mathbf{C}. \] Show that \(\hat f(z)\) is an entire function.

Problems

In problems 2-5, \(m\) stands for Lebesgue measure on \(\mathbb{R}\).

  1. Prove that there exists a real valued function \(f\), defined on a neighborhood of the origin in \(\mathbb{R}^2\), such that

    \[ f(0,0)=0 \text{ and } xyf(x,y)^2 - 2f(x,y)^4 - f(x,y) = x^2y^2. \]

  2. Suppose that \(f(t)\) and \(tf(t)\) are in \(L^1(\mathbb{R},dm)\). Let

    \[ g(x)=\int_{-\infty}^{\infty} f(t)\sin(xt)\,dm(t). \]

    Prove that \(g\) is differentiable, and find \(g'(x)\).

  3. Let \(f \in L^1([0,1],dm)\), and for all Borel subsets \(B\) of \(\mathbb{R}\), define \(v(B)=m(f^{-1}(B))\). Prove that for all bounded Borel functions \(Y\) on \(\mathbb{R}\),

    \[ \int_{[0,1]} Y(f(x))\,dm(x)=\int_{\mathbb{R}} Y(x)\,dv(x). \]

  4. Let \(g(x)=x^2\), and consider the linear map \(T:L^1([0,1],dm)\to \mathbb{R}\) defined by

    \[ Tf=\int_{[0,1]} g(x)f(x)\,dm(x). \]

    Recall that

    \[ \|T\| \equiv \sup \left\{ |Tf| : f \in L^1([0,1],dm), \|f\|_1 \le 1 \right\}. \]

    Compute \(\|T\|\). Note. Verify your computation - do not just quote the Riesz representation theorem.

  5. Let \(f(x)=\frac{1}{1+x^2}\) and \(g(x)=e^x\). Let \(\mathcal{C}\equiv \{f^{-1}(B): B \text{ Borel } \subset \mathbb{R}\}\). For each \(E \in \mathcal{C}\), let \(v_{\mathcal{C}}(E)=\int_E g(x)\,dm(x)\), and \(m_{\mathcal{C}}(E)=m(E)\).

    1. Prove \(B \in \mathcal{C}\) if and only if \(B\) is Borel and \(B=-B\).
    2. Verify that \(v_{\mathcal{C}}\) is absolutely continuous with respect to \(m_{\mathcal{C}}\), on the \(\sigma\)-algebra \(\mathcal{C}\).
    3. Verify that \(g\) is not \(\mathcal{C}\)-measurable.
    4. Compute the Radon-Nikodym derivative of \(v_{\mathcal{C}}\) w.r.t. \(m_{\mathcal{C}}\).

  6. Evaluate \(\int_{\gamma} \frac{dz}{(z^2+4)^2}\) for each of the following \(\gamma\). Be sure to state clearly which results you are using to evaluate the integral.

    1. \(\gamma(t)=-i+\frac{1}{2}e^{it},\ 0\le t\le 2\pi\)
    2. \(\gamma(t)=-i+4e^{it},\ 0\le t\le 2\pi\)
    1. Find a linear fractional transformation which maps the upper half plane onto the interior of the unit circle.
    2. Explicitly describe the image of the first quadrant of the unit circle under the above transformation.

  8. Evaluate \(\int_{-\infty}^{\infty} \frac{1+x^2}{1+x^4}\,dx\). Explain your work clearly, and justify your evaluation.

  9. Find the Laurent series expansion for \(f(z)=\frac{z^2}{z^2-9}\) valid on the annulus \(2<|z-1|<4\).

    1. Prove or give a counter example to the following. There is a monic polynomial \(p(z)\) of degree \(n\) and an \(R>0\) such that \(|p(z)|<R^n\) whenever \(|z|=R\).

DEPARTMENT OF MATHEMATICS, UNIVERSITY OF GEORGIA

PRELIMINARY EXAMINATIONS—SPRING 2000

Instructions

No aids.

Do all questions.

Questions will be weighted equally.

Problems

  1. Let \(X\) be a metric space and \(\{x_n\}_1^\infty\) a convergent sequence in \(X\) with limit \(x_0\). Prove that the set \(C = \{x_0, x_1, x_2, \cdots\}\) is compact.

  2. Let \(n\) be a positive integer and \(0 < \theta < \pi\). Prove that

    \[ \frac{1}{2\pi i}\int_{|z|=2}\frac{z^n}{1-2z\cos\theta+z^2}\,dz = \frac{\sin(n\theta)}{\sin\theta}, \]

    where the circle \(|z|=2\) is oriented counterclockwise.

  3. Suppose that \(f\) is an entire function such that \(|f(z)| \leq C|z|^{1/2}\) for \(|z|\) sufficiently large. (\(C\) is a constant). What can you conclude about the form of \(f\)? Give a proof. (Hint: Cauchy integral formula).

  4. Evaluate \(\int_{-\infty}^{\infty}\frac{e^{-itx}}{a^2+x^2}\,dx\) via residues, where \(a > 0\). Justify every step.

    1. Is the following a Banach space (with respect to a suitable norm)?

      \[ B=\{f:\mathbb{R}\to\mathbb{R}\text{ s.t. }f\text{ continuous, and }\lim_{|x|\to\infty}f(x)=0\}. \]

      Justify your answer.

    2. Suppose \(f\) is continuous, and such that \(\sup |f\cdot g| \leq C \sup |g|\) for all \(g \in B\). Prove that \(|f| \leq C\).

    1. What is the dual of \(L^3(\mathbb{R})\)? Give a proof.

    2. Exhibit an element of the dual of \(\ell^\infty\) that is not in \(\ell^1\).

    1. Show that if \(f \in L^{p_1}(\mathbb{R}) \cap L^{p_2}(\mathbb{R})\), then \(f \in L^p(\mathbb{R})\) for all \(p_1 \leq p \leq p_2\).

    2. Produce a function \(f\) such that \(f \in L^p(\mathbb{R})\) only when \(p = 2\).

  8. Suppose that \(h \in C^1[0,1]\) and \(\nu\) is a finite Borel measure on \([0,1]\). Let \(G(x)=\nu([0,x])\). Prove the following integration by parts formula:

    \[ \int_0^1 h(x)\,d\nu(x)=h(1)G(1)-\int_0^1 h'(x)G(x)\,dx \]

    (Hint: Fubini’s theorem.)

  9. Find a measure \(\mu\), singular with respect to Lebesgue measure, such that \(\mu(I) > 0\) for every non-empty interval \(I\).

  10. Prove that there is no one-to-one conformal map of the punctured disc \(G=\{z \in \mathbb{C}:0<|z|<1\}\) onto the annulus \(A=\{z \in \mathbb{C}:1<|z|<2\}\).

Spring, 1999

Name Student Id. No.

Instructions

There are ten problems in total. Please work as many problems as possible. Use a separate sheet of paper to do each problem and show all your work.

Problems

  1. State and prove one of the following theorems:

    1. Lusin's Theorem
    2. Egoroff's Theorem
    3. Fubini's Theorem
    4. Lebesgue Dominated Convergence Theorem
    5. The Riesz Representation Theorem for \(L_p\).

  2. Show that the set of all discontinuity points of a monotone function is countable.

  3. Suppose that \(f_n, n = 1,2,3,\cdots\) converge to \(f\) in measure and \(g_n, n = 1,2,3,\cdots,\) converge to \(g\) in measure. Here, \(f_n\) converges to \(f\) in measure if, for any \(\epsilon > 0\).

    \[ \lim_{n \rightarrow +\infty} \text{ measure }\left(\left\{x,\left|f_n(x)-f(x)\right| > \epsilon\right\}\right)=0 \]

    Suppose that both \(f\) and \(g\) are bounded almost everywhere. Show that \(f_n g_n\) converges to \(fg\) in measure.

  4. Suppose that \(f\) is Lebesgue integrable on \([a,b]\). Show that

    \[ \lim_{n \rightarrow +\infty} \int_a^b f(x)\,|\cos(nx)|\,dx=\int_a^b f(x)\,dx. \]

  5. Show that if \(\{f_n\}\) converges to \(f\) in \(L_2(a,b)\) weakly and \(\|f_n\|_2\) converges to \(\|f\|_2\), then \(f_n\) converges to \(f\) strongly in \(L_2(a,b)\).

  6. State and prove one of the following theorems:

    1. Cauchy's Integral Theorem
    2. Morera's Theorem
    3. Rouche's Theorem
    4. Weierstrass' Theorem
    5. Mittag-Leffler's Theorem

    1. Find an analytic function \(f(z)\) at \(z=0\) satisfying \(f(1/n)=\frac{n}{n+1},\ n=1,2,\cdots\). Show that such an analytic function is unique.

    2. Can you find an analytic function \(f(z)\) at \(z=0\) satisfying \(f\!\left(\frac{1}{2n-1}\right)=0,\ f\!\left(\frac{1}{2n}\right)=\frac{1}{2n}\) for \(n=1,2,\cdots,\)? Give your function if you can or give your reason if you can't.

  8. Compute the Laurent series of function \(f(z)=\frac{1}{(z-1)(z-2)}\) in annulus \(\{z:1<|z|<2\}\) and \(\{z:2<|z|<\infty\}\).

  9. Use the residue theorem to compute the following integral:

    \[ I=\int_0^\infty \frac{\ln x}{(1+x)^3}\,dx. \]

  10. Find explicitly a conformal mapping of half disk \(\{z:|z|<1,\operatorname{Im}(z)>0\}\) onto the upper half plane.
